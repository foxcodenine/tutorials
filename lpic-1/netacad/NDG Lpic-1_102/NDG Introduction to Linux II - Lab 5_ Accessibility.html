5.1 Step 1

Each lab has been written using a fresh virtual machine, meaning we have reset the virtual machines before each use. If you have already used the GUI virtual machines, it may be advantageous to reset them, otherwise the results you see when performing the steps of this lab may be slightly different from what is displayed here.
For this lab, you will be using the CentOS PC virtual machine.

Select the CentOS PC by clicking on the CentOS PC tab in the virtual machine window on the right side of the screen.

First, login to the root account. When prompted for the password, use the root password netlab123:

CentOS Linux 7 (Core)
Kernel 3.10.0-1062.el7.x86_64 on an x86_64
 
centos login: root
Password:
[root@centos ~]#

Switch to the GUI by bringing the system to runlevel 5 using the init 5 command:

init 5
[root@centos ~]# init 5

Next, login to the sysadmin account, again using the password netlab123.

5.2 Step 2

Click the Application menu, then click Sundry and then click Orca to open the screen reader:

5.3 Step 3

After enabling the Orca screen reader, click the Preferences button in the dialog box to view the Orca settings.

Our virtual environment will not have sound enabled. However, on a live system, you would be able to use the screen reader and hear the text being read by the screen reader.

Click the x button on the Orca Preferences window and the Orca Screen Reader dialog box to close the Orca screen Reader.

5.4 Step 4

Click the Application menu, then click System Tools and Settings.

5.5 Step 5

Under Settings, click on Universal Access. Under the Universal Access menu, click on Typing Assist (AccessX) in the Typing section.

Click the switch to ON to enable Sticky Keys and place a checkmark next to Disable if two keys are pressed together.

5.6 Step 6

Open the text editor to test the Sticky Keys settings. To open a blank document using the text editor, Click on Applications > Accessories > Text Editor:

5.7 Step 7

Once in the editor, type the following:

Shift+a

A window should appear stating that Sticky Keys has been turned off:

Recall that the Disable if two keys are pressed together option was checked under the Sticky Keys menu in the previous step. Select the Leave Off option then type the following:

Shift+b Shift+c

This time you should be able to press two keys keys together to type the capital letters B and C.

5.8 Step 8

Go back to the Universal Access settings and turn the Sticky Keys feature back on. To return to the Typing Assist window in Universal Access, click the Settings tab at the bottom of the screen:

5.9 Step 9

In the Typing Assist window, click the switch to turn on the Sticky Keys and ensure that the Disable if two keys are pressed together option is still checked:

5.10 Step 10

Return to the Text Editor by clicking on the Untitled Document 1 tab at the bottom of the page.

In the Untitled Document 1 window, press the following keys one at a time:

Enter
Shift 
a 
Shift 
b 
Shift 
c

The document should display the letters ABC. This occurs because this feature allows you to enter a Shift+A or Ctrl+A by pressing one key at a time, instead of needing to hold down the Shift or Ctrl key while pressing the letter A.

5.11 Step 11

Click on the Settings tab at the bottom of the screen to return to the Universal Access settings.

In the Typing Assist window, click the switch to enable Slow Keys, then drag the Acceptance Delay all of the way to the right (Long).

5.12 Step 12

Click the Untitled Document 1 tab to return to the Text Editor. In the Untitled Document 1 window, press the following key:

d
Note that no letter appears as the acceptance delay is too long for our virtual environment to recognize and accept the key. However, on a standard system, the letter d will appear a few seconds after the key is pressed.

5.13 Step 13

Click the Settings tab at the bottom of the screen to return to the Universal Access settings. In the Typing Assist window, under Slow Keys, drag the Acceptance Delay all of the way to the left (Short).

5.14 Step 14

Click the Untitled Document 1 tab to return to the Text Editor. In the Untitled Document 1 window, press the following keys one at a time until a character appears in the test area (If you make a mistake, press and hold the Backspace key):

d e f

The document should display ABCdef. This feature is useful when someone consistently hits keys accidentally since it prevents these accidentally typed keys from being displayed as characters.

5.15 Step 15

Click the Settings tab to return to the Typing Assist window and click the switch to turn off Slow Keys.

5.16 Step 16

Under Bounce Keys, click the switch to enable Bounce Keys.

5.17 Step 17

Click the Untitled Document 1 tab to return to the Text Editor. In the Untitled Document 1 window, press the following keys quickly:

g g g h h h i i i

The document should display ABCdefghi. The characters g, h and i should appear only once because when you have the Ignore fast duplicate keypresses option enabled, multiple fast keystrokes of the same key are ignored (only the first is displayed).

5.18 Step 18

Click the Settings tab at the bottom of the screen to return to the Typing Assist window and click the switch to turn off Bounce Keys:

5.19 Step 19

The Toggle Keys feature initially provided audio feedback when keyboard modifier keys, like Shift, Alt, and Ctrl, are pressed. To find the so-called Toggle Keys settings, click the Audio Feedback button. To enable Toggle Keys, under Sticky Keys, check the box for Beep when a modifier key is pressed, which will enable audio feedback:

Now when an alert sound is issued by the system, the screen will also flash. This is useful for when the sound is turned off and for individuals who have hearing issues.

Click the the x button to exit the Typing Assist window.

5.20 Step 20

To test the Toggle Keys, the visual alerts will need to be turned on. To turn on the Visual Alerts, in the Universal Access window under Hearing, click Visual Alerts. Next, click the switch to ON to enable visual alerts.

Once Visual Alerts is enabled, select the Flash the window tile option and then click on the Test flash button to test the visual alert:

After clicking on the Test flash button, the window should flash black for a second and then go back to normal.

5.21 Step 21

Verify that the visual alerts work for Toggle Keys when typing. Click on the Untitled Document 1 tab at the bottom of the screen. In the Untitled Document 1 window, click the following keys:

Shift
Ctrl

The screen should flash after each modifier key is pressed.

5.22 Step 22

Click the Settings tab at the bottom of the screen to return to the Universal Access window. Click the x button to close the Visual Alerts window. Next, in the Universal Access window, under Pointing and Clicking, click on Click Assist.

5.23 Step 23

In the Click Assist window, click the switch to ON to enable Simulated Secondary Click, adjust the Acceptance Delay to increase the amount of time needed to hold the primary button to trigger the secondary button.

Next, click on the Settings tab and the Untitled Document 1 tab to minimize the windows and return to the Desktop. On the Desktop, click and hold the primary mouse button (left-click) on the Home folder. Once the mouse completely turned gray, release the mouse button. This will trigger the secondary click, which will open the Home folder drop-down menu.

5.24 Step 24

Click on the Settings tab at the bottom of the screen to return to Universal Access. In the Click Assist window, click the switch to OFF to disable Simulated Secondary Click. Next, under Hover Click, click the switch to ON to enable Hover Click.

A select box then appears allowing you to control what happens when using the Hover Click:

5.25 Step 25

Move the mouse pointer over the x button on the Hover click select box and "dwell" there for a few seconds. The cursor should change color from black to gray, which indicates it is time to make the gesture. The select box should close, as if you had clicked the x button.

In the Click Assist window, hover over the switch and keep the mouse there to turn OFF the Hover click feature.

This lab is complete.
